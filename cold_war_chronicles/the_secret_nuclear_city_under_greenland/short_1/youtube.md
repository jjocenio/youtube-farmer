## Video Description

### Hook (first 150 characters — all that shows before "more")
The US secretly built a nuclear base under Greenland's ice and planned 600 missiles there. Denmark had no idea. The waste is still there.

### Full Description
Camp Century looked like an Arctic research station. Underneath: a full military base carved into the Greenland ice sheet — running water, a hospital, a movie theater, powered by a portable nuclear reactor.

The real plan was Project Iceworm: 4,000 km of tunnels, 600 nuclear missiles on rail cars moving continuously so the Soviets could never target them. Every major Soviet city in range.

Denmark's prime minister was privately briefed and said nothing to his own parliament. The 1951 Defense Agreement covered facilities — not missiles.

The project was abandoned in 1966 when the ice sheet moved and fractured the tunnels. The nuclear waste left behind — reactor coolant, fuel, 200,000 liters of sewage — is still under Greenland's ice. As the ice melts, it will resurface.

Full 20-minute documentary on the channel. The base, the missiles, and what's still frozen beneath the ice.
