# Google Veo Prompt — Crypto AG Trailer Short (20–30s)

## Prompt

Generate a 25-second cinematic trailer-style short video with NO dialogue and NO voiceover. The video uses rapid-cut editing with 8–10 distinct shots, each lasting 2–3 seconds, building from slow and ominous to urgent and paranoid. All footage should be stylized as historical archival material: Kodachrome 60s–70s film grain, cold desaturated blues and amber highlights, cinematic noir lighting, heavy shadows. The overall aesthetic is "espionage thriller meets Cold War documentary."

**Shot sequence:**

**[0:00–0:02] — COLD OPEN**
Extreme close-up, macro lens: a brass mechanical cipher machine rotor spinning in slow motion against a pure black background. A single desk lamp catches the spinning metal. No sound except a faint metallic click. Fade in from black.

**[0:02–0:05] — ESTABLISHING DREAD**
Wide shot, low angle: an unremarkable 1970s suburban Swiss office building under an overcast Alpine sky. Grey concrete, small windows, nothing remarkable. A lone suited figure exits through a glass door and walks away without looking back. The camera slowly pushes in on the building's facade. Kodachrome cold tones.

**[0:05–0:08] — THE SECRET**
Overhead cinematic shot: two pairs of hands meeting across a mahogany conference table — one set slides a document forward, the other places a banded stack of cash beside it. Neither person's face is visible. A single overhead lamp casts deep shadows. The document has a CIA seal partially visible. Quick cut.

**[0:08–0:11] — WORLD MAP REVEAL**
Animated top-down view of a Cold War-era globe, slowly rotating. One by one, glowing amber dots appear on countries across every continent — Iran, Argentina, Italy, Libya, the Vatican, India, Pakistan — spreading like an infection until 120+ nations are marked. The globe is rendered in muted Kodachrome earth tones. No narration.

**[0:11–0:14] — THE BOMB**
Fast cut to: a West Berlin street at night, 1986. Emergency vehicle lights strobe red and blue across a damaged nightclub facade. Police tape. Broken glass on the cobblestones. Quick handheld camera movement. Cold, grainy, urgent.

**[0:14–0:17] — THE INTERROGATION**
Tight shot: a stark interrogation room — bare concrete walls, single hanging bulb, a visibly exhausted man seated across from two uniformed figures whose faces are in shadow. The bulb swings slightly. The man's hands rest flat on the metal table. Silence except for a distant door echo. Kodachrome institutional cold blue.

**[0:17–0:20] — THE WHISTLEBLOWER**
Medium shot: a 1994 television broadcast studio. A man sits in silhouette against an intensely bright backlit screen. His face is completely hidden in darkness. A distortion effect ripples across the image as if from a broadcast signal. The word "MY LIFE MIGHT BE IN DANGER" appears on the screen beneath him in white text, as if a subtitle.

**[0:20–0:23] — THE REVEAL**
Fast cut to: a Washington Post front page filling the frame, the headline reading "The Intelligence Coup of the Century." The camera slowly zooms into the headline text until it fills the screen. Dramatic deep string music sting. Then — hard cut to black.

**[0:23–0:27] — TITLE CARD + CTA**
Black screen. White text fades in, centered, in a clean sans-serif font:

Line 1 (large): **THE FULL STORY**
Line 2 (medium, 0.5s delay): **IS ON THE CHANNEL**
Line 3 (small, subtle, 0.5s delay): *Link in description*

Hold for 3 seconds. Fade to black.

---

**Music direction:** Begins with near-silence and a single low piano note. Builds through sparse strings to a tight, urgent brass sting at [0:20]. Cuts to silence at the black screen. No licensed music — generate original tension score in the style of a Cold War spy film (Hans Zimmer / Jóhann Jóhannsson aesthetic).

**Aspect ratio:** 9:16 (vertical, optimized for YouTube Shorts, Instagram Reels, TikTok)

**Color grade:** Consistent Kodachrome-era desaturation throughout — no bright colors, no modern vibrancy. Blues and ambers only. Heavy film grain at 35mm texture.

**No text overlays during the main shots** (except the subtitle in the whistleblower shot and the final title card). Let the visuals carry the tension.
