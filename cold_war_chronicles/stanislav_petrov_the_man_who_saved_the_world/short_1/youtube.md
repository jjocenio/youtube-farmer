## Video Description

### Hook (first 150 characters — all that shows before "more")
On Sept 26 1983, one Soviet officer had 5 minutes to decide if World War III had started — alone, no proof, against protocol.

### Full Description
On September 26th, 1983, Stanislav Petrov sat alone at a Soviet early-warning console and watched five American nuclear missiles appear on his screen. Protocol said: report it immediately. He didn't.

He called it a false alarm — with no evidence, against every rule — and waited 23 minutes to find out if he was right.

He was. A satellite had mistaken sunlight reflecting off clouds for missile launches. He never received a commendation. He was quietly reprimanded for not filling in his logbook.

Full 21-minute documentary on the channel. Every detail of the night that was almost the last night.
