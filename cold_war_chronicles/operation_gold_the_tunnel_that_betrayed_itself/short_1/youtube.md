## Thumbnail Strategy

### Visual Concept
The most striking visual in the Short is the moment the Soviet flashlight beam hits the sealed steel tunnel door underground — an unexpected wall where there should be earth. Crop to vertical: the POV shot looking at the anti-personnel door with faint electric glow leaking from its seam, the flashlight beam cutting across it from above. In the upper dark area: text overlay. The viewer doesn't know what they're looking at — and that's the hook.

A single flashlight beam cuts diagonally across the door from upper left, catching the rivet heads and weld seams in stark relief. Walls: steel-ringed, glistening with condensation. Floor: wet concrete and dark mud. Total darkness above the door. Color grade: cold desaturated blues and blacks, the only light sources the thin electric glow from the door gap and the flashlight beam. Atmosphere: discovery of something hidden, intrusion, dread. Kodachrome 60s film grain, historical archival photo style. Upper quarter of frame dark negative space for text overlay.

## Video Description

### Hook (first 150 characters — all that shows before "more")
The CIA spent 3 years digging a tunnel under Berlin to tap Soviet cables. The KGB knew before the first shovel went in.

### Full Description
Operation Gold was a joint CIA–MI6 operation: a tunnel dug beneath the American sector in Berlin to tap three Soviet military cables. They listened for 11 months and 11 days.

The problem: George Blake, the British intelligence officer who sat in every planning meeting and wrote the minutes, was a KGB agent. Moscow had the plans before construction began.

The KGB chose silence to protect Blake. The 1956 "discovery" by a Soviet repair crew was theater — staged at the exact moment Blake was safely out of reach.

The CIA called it a successful intelligence operation. They never knew they'd been inside a trap.

Full 22-minute documentary on the channel. The tunnel, Blake, and how the CIA spied on itself for nearly a year.
