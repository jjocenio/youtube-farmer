## Thumbnail Strategy

### Visual Concept
The hook frame is the Short's pivot moment: the split frame of NATO officers laughing over a map while Soviet generals sit in grim silence. Crop to the Soviet generals side for the thumbnail — three uniformed officers around a table under a single overhead lamp, expressions unreadable, readiness reports spread before them. In the upper third: a short text line. The contrast between their gravity and what viewers know was a "training drill" is the emotional punch.

Vertical composition: three Soviet military generals in dark uniform seated at a heavy conference table, viewed from a slightly low angle — stern expressions, eyes fixed on papers spread before them, one man's hand flat on the table as if making a decision. Single harsh overhead lamp casting deep shadows below their brows, green baize table surface visible at bottom of frame, a large European wall map partially visible in the blurred background. Color grade: cold desaturated tones, amber from the overhead lamp, deep shadow everywhere else. Atmosphere: absolute gravity, a decision already made. Kodachrome 1980s grain, historical archival photo style. Upper quarter of frame left as dark negative space for text overlay.

## Video Description

### Hook (first 150 characters — all that shows before "more")
In 1983, NATO ran a drill so realistic the Soviets loaded nuclear warheads onto aircraft. NATO had no idea any of it was happening.

### Full Description
Able Archer 83 was a NATO training exercise simulating a full nuclear release sequence. The Soviets, already convinced the US was planning a first strike, read it as the real thing.

They loaded nuclear warheads onto bombers. 75 missile launchers vanished into Eastern European forests. A submarine went dark under the Arctic ice. 11,000 warheads went on maximum alert.

The war that almost happened by accident ended only when one East German spy passed documents to British intelligence — who passed them to Reagan.

Full 20-minute documentary on the channel. Every decision. Every escalation. How close it actually got.
