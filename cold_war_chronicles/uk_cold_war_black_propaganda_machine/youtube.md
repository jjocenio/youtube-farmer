### SEO Paragraph
The Information Research Department (IRD) was Britain's longest-running Cold War propaganda unit — a secret Foreign Office department that conducted approximately 350 black propaganda operations between 1951 and 1975, forging Soviet documents, creating fictitious anti-communist organisations, and laundering government money through the BBC and Reuters to shape international news coverage. Founded in 1948 by Christopher Mayhew and Ernest Bevin, the IRD operated across Africa, Asia, Latin America, and the Middle East, targeting newly independent nations during the Cold War decolonisation era. Its most shocking chapter: George Orwell, author of *Nineteen Eighty-Four*, provided a secret informant list to an IRD agent — while his novel *Animal Farm* was simultaneously being distributed as British government propaganda in 16 language translations. Exposed by Guardian journalist David Leigh in 1977 and dissolved by Foreign Secretary David Owen, the IRD's techniques of front organisations, forged documents, and institutional media laundering constitute the direct blueprint for modern information warfare. If you're interested in Cold War history, British intelligence, MI6 covert operations, propaganda history, or the untold stories of the espionage war between East and West, this is essential viewing.
